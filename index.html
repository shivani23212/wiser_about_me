A Typical Day...

... is never the same!

Between being a university student, helping out at home during the holidays and attending career, charity and friends' events, I look forward to the variety every day and season of the year brings, since I believe you can’t grow without change!

Although each day provides a new challenge, I do enjoy including several staple activities in my daily routine to remain motivated, grounded and content. As you will discover, these include hobbies (such as strength training, learning the ukulele or reading), learning (be this through lectures or study sessions with my friends) and ensuring I am looking after my mental and physical health through sleeping and eating right.

Below I have outlined how an average day might pan out – depending on my location and how packed my schedule is!

A day in the life...

A day at Uni

I wake up, get ready (pray, shower, brush my teeth etc) and check my diary. My first lecture’s scheduled at 9:00am – it’s currently 7:15am and I have some time. I quickly make a packed breakfast, lunch (don’t forget the coffee!), pack my bag for the day and head out to the gym. After a quick session it’s now 8:40am and I head to campus for the lecture then go to the library to consolidate and revise for the upcoming test. After this I apply to a couple of internships, complete any admin needed and check my diary for tomorrow (in case of any upcoming events I need to prepare for tonight), at around 5pm I make my way to meet my friends from Hindu Society and unwind by the best means – gossip! By 7pm I’m home and I either work a bit more on coursework or video call my family before watching YouTube with some dinner. At around 10:30pm I get ready for bed and go to sleep!

I would wake up at around 8:00am – (still) check my diary for any upcoming meetings through the day, then head to the local leisure centre at 8:45am. After dinner, I go to sleep at around 12:00am – looking forward to the next day.

Organisation

Since I enjoy a range of activities throughout the day – I keep myself in check using my (sacred) diary. In this I write up the timings of any lectures, society meetings, one-off commitments and planned events for the day. I usually enter in these commitments during the weekend to make sure I am organised for the upcoming week. In this diary I also note down any assignments due – and write up touchpoints on certain days (such as 4 weeks before due date, 2 weeks before, 3 days before and so on) to ensure I am on track with submitting my work.
